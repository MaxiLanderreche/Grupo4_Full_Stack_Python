Urban art: graffiti and murals


A global trend that finds its place in the streets of Buenos Aires. The city walls house one of the most active urban art movements in Latin America. Artists come from all over the world to turn gray walls into an open-air museum.


Did you know?

Urban art in the city has been regulated since 2009 in the muralism law, a culture that developed in the streets from very early times, following political fluctuations. Interventions must have the approval of the property owner.
